Why choose Bethune College?

Established in 1879, Bethune College ushered in, for the women of the 19th century, a new era of enlightenment and edification through education. This is a government College, under the aegis of the Dept. of Higher Education, West Bengal. Accredited in 200g with the 'A' grade and having acquired the coveted status of College with Potential for Excellence (CPE) by UGC and recognised by DST-FIST. Bethune College has celebrated 143 long years of tireless striving for knowledge which enriches the mind and enhances the soul.

Distinguished Alumni
Image Name Field
Chandramukhi Bose
(1860-1944)		 Educationist & Scholar
One of the first two female graduates of the British Empire, she passed the BA examination from the University of Calcutta in 1882.
Kadambini Ganguly (Nee Bose)
(1861-1923)		 Doctor
She was one of the first two female graduates of the British Empire and also the first Indian woman to practice medicine in India.
Kamini Roy
(1864-1933)		 Poet and Author
She was a Bengali poet, social worker and feminist in British India. She was the first woman honours graduate in British India.
Lady Abala Bose
(1865-1951)		 Educationist, Social and Political Activist
She was known for her efforts in women's education and her contribution towards helping widows.
Sarala Devi Chaudhurani (Nee Ghosal)
(1872-1945)		 Educationist, Social and Political Activist
She was an educationist and political activist, who founded Bharat Stree Mahamandal in Allahabad in 1910. This was the first women's organization in India, primarily to promote female education.
Leela Roy (nee Nag)
(1900-1970)		 Educationist, Social and Political Activist
She was a progressive Indian woman politician and reformer, and a close associate of Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose.
Kamala Dasgupta
(1907-2000)		 Political Activist and revolutionary
She was an Indian freedom fighter.
Pritilata Waddedar
(1911-1932)		 Revolutionary
She was a Bengali revolutionary nationalist from the Indian subcontinent who was influential in the Indian independence movement.
Kalpana Joshi (Dutt)
(1913 - 1995)		 Revolutionary
She was an Indian independence movement activist and a member of the armed independence movement led by Surya Sen, which carried out the Chittagong armoury raid in 1930.
Sadhana Bose (nee Sen)
(1914 - 1973)		 Actress
She was a renowned danseuse cum actress.
Dr. (Prof.)Asima Chatterjee
(1917-2006)		 Academician
She was an Indian organic chemist noted for her work in the fields of organic chemistry and phytomedicine. She was the first woman to receive a Doctorate of Science from an Indian university.
Shobha Sen
(1923-2017)		 Eminent theatre and film personality
She was a Bengali theatre and film actress.
Krishna Das
(1959-)		 Archer
She represented India in the Asian Games twice, and in three Asian Archery meets. She was the first archer to receive the Indian Government's Arjuna Award to recognise outstanding sportspersons in 1984.
Bratati Bandyopadhyay
(1966-)		 Elocutionist
She an internationally acclaimed Bengali language elocutionist.
Dr. (Prof.) Neena Gupta
(1984-)		 Mathematician
